Layer 1

Daily Rhythm

Three fixed anchors: Morning Boot (20 min), Deep Work Blocks (2×90 min), Evening Shutdown (15 min). Everything else slots around these.

Layer 2

Weekly Architecture

Monday Planning + Friday Review as hard bookends. Themed days reduce context switching cost by ~40%. Admin batched to Tuesday PM.

Layer 3

Project Templates

Every new project starts from a pre-built scaffold: goals, constraints, milestones, daily progress log, retrospective. Zero blank-page tax.

Layer 4

Capture & Review Loops

Weekly 45-min review processes all inboxes, updates OKRs, pre-loads next week. Monthly 2hr review recalibrates quarterly direction.

Core Principle: Your system should answer three questions instantly at any moment — What should I be doing right now? What did I commit to this week? Am I on track toward my 90-day goal? If it can't, the system isn't complete.

Design Principles

Operating Constraints

Rules that prevent the system from collapsing under load.

1
Capture Everything, Process Once
One inbox (physical + digital). Nothing lives in your head. Processing happens at fixed times, not continuously. Continuous processing destroys focus.
2
Time-Box Before You To-Do
Tasks go on the calendar, not a list. A list is a wish. A calendar block is a commitment. If it's not scheduled, it probably won't happen.
3
Protect the First 90 Minutes
No email, Slack, or meetings before your first deep work block completes. Your best cognitive hours belong to your most important work.
4
Distinguish Doing from Deciding
Planning sessions are for deciding. Work blocks are for doing. Never mix them — deciding during execution fragments both.
5
Debt-Free Closure
Every day ends with a shutdown ritual. Open loops create background anxiety. The shutdown converts open loops into scheduled actions, freeing RAM.

Standard Operating Procedures

Daily Execution Protocols

Each SOP is designed to run on autopilot. Read once, internalize, then execute from habit.

01
Morning Boot Sequence
Duration: 20 min · Trigger: Wake + 30 min · Frequency: Daily
  • 1
    Physical Activation (5 min)
    10 min walk OR 20 push-ups + cold water on face. Goal: cortisol spike without phone. No screens until this is done.
  • 2
    Daily Preview (5 min)
    Open calendar only. Identify today's 3 MIT (Most Important Tasks). Write them on a physical index card. Do not open email.
    MIT-1: [the thing that moves the needle most] MIT-2: [second priority] MIT-3: [third, or a batch of small things] AVOID today: [specific distraction to pre-commit against]
  • 3
    Pre-Load Deep Work Context (5 min)
    Open exactly the files/tabs needed for DW Block 1. Close everything else. Set a 90-min timer. Phone in another room.
  • 4
    Intention Sentence (2 min)
    Write one sentence: "Today I will have made meaningful progress if _______." This is your success criterion. Check it at shutdown.
02
Deep Work Block Protocol
Duration: 90 min · Sessions: 2/day · Frequency: Mon–Fri
  • 1
    Entry Ritual (3 min)
    Same ritual every time: make drink, put on one specific playlist (instrumental only), write the micro-goal for this 90-min session at top of scratch doc.
    Session goal: [one specific deliverable, e.g. "draft section 2.3" not "work on paper"] Time-box: 90 min ending at ___:___ Success = [concrete output]
  • 2
    Distraction Capture (during)
    Keep a sticky note beside keyboard. Any thought that pulls attention ("should check X", "need to email Y") goes on the note, not in your head or browser. Review after session ends.
  • 3
    Pomodoro Override Rule
    If in flow state when 25-min pomodoro rings: IGNORE it, keep going. The 90-min outer limit is the real boundary. Pomodoro is a floor, not a ceiling.
  • 4
    Exit Note (5 min)
    Critical: before stopping, write 3 lines. This is the "warm handoff" to your future self — eliminates re-entry cost next session.
    Completed: [what actually got done] Stuck on: [specific blocker if any] Next action: [exact first step to continue]
03
Daily Shutdown Ritual
Duration: 15 min · Trigger: Last work hour · Frequency: Daily
  • 1
    Capture Sweep (5 min)
    Process all physical notes, Slack mentions, email subjects. Don't respond — just capture as tasks in your system. Goal: zero open loops in your head.
  • 2
    Calendar Check Tomorrow (3 min)
    Scan tomorrow's schedule. Pre-decide: is anything scheduled that shouldn't be? Add any prep time needed. Confirm tomorrow's MIT-1 is blocked.
  • 3
    Wins + Intentions Log (5 min)
    Write 3 lines in your daily log. Not for motivation — for calibration data. These become the raw material for weekly review.
    WIN: [one thing that worked] LEARN: [one thing to adjust] TOMORROW: [the single thing I'm most focused on]
  • 4
    Shutdown Complete Phrase
    Say out loud or write: "Shutdown complete." Sounds silly, works clinically. Creates a psychological boundary between work mode and rest mode. Work is done.

Theme Logic

Why Themed Days Work

Context switch cost is real — studies show 15–25 min recovery per switch. Collab day (Wed) batches all social cognitive load. Deep days (Tue/Thu) have no meetings before noon by policy.

Email Policy

Batch Processing Only

Email twice daily: 11:30 AM and 4:00 PM. Auto-responder if needed: "I check email at 11:30 and 16:00. For urgent matters, call." Most urgency is manufactured.

Meeting Rules

Meeting Hygiene

All meetings have a written agenda sent 24h before. Default 30 min, not 60. Every meeting ends with written next actions + owner. No agenda = you decline or convert to async.

Feedback Loop

Weekly Review Protocol

The most important system ritual. Skipping it is how systems die. 45 minutes every Friday. Non-negotiable.

WR
Friday Weekly Review — Full Protocol
Duration: 45 min · Trigger: Friday 14:00 · Output: Next week pre-loaded
  • 1
    Capture Clear (5 min)
    Process all open inboxes to zero: physical inbox, email inbox, notes app, Slack DMs. Everything either becomes a task, gets deleted, or gets filed. If it takes <2 min, do it now.
  • 2
    Review the Week (10 min)
    Read back through this week's daily logs. What actually happened vs what was planned? Calculate your MIT completion rate. Be honest, not harsh.
    MITs planned: ___ | MITs completed: ___ | Rate: ___% Biggest win: ___ Biggest miss: ___ Pattern I noticed: ___
  • 3
    Project Status Sweep (10 min)
    For each active project: update status, check if on track vs target date. Identify any project that needs escalated attention next week. Archive anything stalled for 3+ weeks.
    For each project: [Name] → Status: [On Track / At Risk / Behind / Done] Progress this week: ___ Next action: ___ by ___
  • 4
    90-Day Goal Check (5 min)
    Review your current 90-day goals (you should have 1–3, not 10). Are you on track? Does this week's work connect to them? If not, something is misaligned.
  • 5
    Next Week Pre-Load (10 min)
    Open next week's Monday Planning template. Fill it out now while context is fresh. Pre-schedule the 2 deep work sessions for your highest priority. This is what makes Monday effortless.
  • 6
    System Health Check (5 min)
    Is the system working? What friction point annoyed you this week? Adjust one thing. Systems that can't evolve die. Small iterative improvements compound dramatically.
    Friction I noticed: ___ System tweak for next week: ___ Energy management note: ___
Monthly Calibration (first Friday of month, +30 min): Review last 4 weekly reviews. Update your 90-day goals if needed. Ask: "Is how I'm spending time aligned with what I actually care about?" This is the only question that matters at the monthly level.

Implementation

How to Activate This System

Don't implement everything at once. Staged rollout over 4 weeks.

W1
Week 1: Install the Daily Anchors Only
Morning boot + shutdown ritual. Nothing else. Do these for 7 days until they're automatic. Don't add templates yet.
W2
Week 2: Add Deep Work Blocks
Block 2×90 min in your calendar as recurring. Treat as meetings you can't cancel. Use the entry/exit note protocol. Track actual output.
W3
Week 3: First Weekly Review
Run the Friday review protocol for the first time. Use the weekly planning template on Monday. Notice what doesn't fit your context and adapt.
W4
Week 4: Full System Live
Add the project scaffold template and calendar theming. The system is now running. Your job from here: do the reviews, resist the urge to over-engineer, and let compounding happen.
